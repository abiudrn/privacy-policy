# AfyaBeat Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 21, 2026

## Overview

AfyaBeat is a mobile application that helps users record, review, and understand blood pressure and pulse readings over time. This Privacy Policy explains what information AfyaBeat may collect, how that information is used, how it may be stored, and the choices available to users.

## Who This Policy Applies To

This Privacy Policy applies to users of the AfyaBeat mobile application and related services operated in connection with the app.

## Information We Collect

AfyaBeat may collect the following categories of information.

### 1. Account and identity information

When a user signs in, AfyaBeat may process:

- name or display name
- email address
- profile photo URL
- account identifier such as Firebase user ID
- authentication method, such as Google sign-in

### 2. Profile information

AfyaBeat may collect or store profile details provided by the user, including:

- age
- gender
- preferred measuring arm

### 3. Health and wellness information entered by the user

AfyaBeat is designed to store health-related information that the user enters into the app, including:

- systolic blood pressure
- diastolic blood pressure
- pulse or heart rate
- date and time of a reading
- blood pressure classification and pulse classification derived from the reading
- notes or symptom information entered by the user, including quick-note options such as feeling tired, anxious, dizzy, or after exercise

### 4. App settings and preferences

AfyaBeat may store user preferences, including:

- morning reminder preference
- weekly summary preference
- dark theme preference
- sign-in completion state
- sign-in method and sign-in email stored locally for app behavior

### 5. Analytics and technical usage information

AfyaBeat uses analytics and diagnostics tooling that may collect technical and usage information such as:

- app events and general usage interactions
- device and app environment metadata
- crash logs
- error details
- route or screen context
- HTTP response metadata when errors are reported

### 6. Local device data

AfyaBeat may store certain information locally on the device using local storage and database technologies, including app state, preferences, and reading-related data needed for app functionality.

## How We Use Information

AfyaBeat may use information for the following purposes:

- to create and manage user accounts
- to authenticate users and keep them signed in
- to personalize the app experience
- to save and display blood pressure and pulse readings
- to classify readings and show interpretation guidance
- to show reading history, recent readings, and weekly trends
- to maintain profile and settings preferences
- to improve app performance and stability
- to detect, troubleshoot, and fix crashes or technical issues
- to understand usage patterns through analytics
- to support data-related features such as reading review or export

## Health-Related Information Notice

AfyaBeat may process blood pressure, pulse, and related wellness notes that users choose to enter. This information may be considered sensitive personal data or health-related information under some laws.

AfyaBeat is intended to help users track readings and understand general classification guidance. It is not a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.

## Where Information Is Stored

Based on the current app architecture, information may be stored in more than one place:

### 1. On the user’s device

AfyaBeat may store data locally using:

- shared preferences
- local SQLite-based storage

This may include app preferences, sign-in state, and locally persisted app data.

### 2. In cloud services

AfyaBeat currently uses Firebase services. Depending on the feature, information may be stored or processed through:

- Firebase Authentication
- Cloud Firestore
- Firebase Analytics
- Firebase Crashlytics

Cloud Firestore is used for user-linked application data such as:

- profile information
- user settings
- blood pressure readings

## Authentication and Sign-In

AfyaBeat currently supports sign-in flows including Google sign-in and account-based authentication through Firebase Authentication.

When users authenticate, AfyaBeat may receive and store profile attributes provided by the authentication provider or Firebase, such as:

- display name
- email address
- photo URL
- unique user ID

## Analytics and Crash Reporting

AfyaBeat currently includes analytics and crash/error reporting tools.

### Firebase Analytics

Firebase Analytics may be used to:

- measure general app usage
- associate certain events with a user ID where configured
- store high-level environment information

### Firebase Crashlytics

Firebase Crashlytics may be used to:

- collect crash reports
- collect error diagnostics
- associate crash logs with a user identifier
- include limited metadata relevant to technical troubleshooting

Based on the current implementation, crash and error reports may include a user email address when a user is signed in. Error reports may also include request or response metadata when the app explicitly logs an error.

## Data Sharing

AfyaBeat does not sell personal information.

AfyaBeat may share or process data through service providers used to operate the app, including:

- Google and Firebase services for authentication, storage, analytics, and crash reporting

Data may also be disclosed:

- if required by applicable law, legal process, or lawful government request
- to protect rights, safety, security, or the integrity of the app
- in connection with a merger, acquisition, financing, restructuring, or sale of assets, subject to applicable legal requirements

## Data Retention

AfyaBeat may retain information for as long as needed to:

- provide the app and its features
- maintain user accounts and reading history
- comply with legal obligations
- resolve disputes
- enforce terms or protect the service

Local data may remain on a device until the user removes the app data, clears app storage, or uninstalls the application. Cloud-stored data may remain until it is deleted by the user or removed by the service operator according to operational needs or legal requirements.

## User Choices and Controls

Depending on the app experience and deployment, users may be able to:

- update profile information
- adjust reminder or summary preferences
- sign out
- manage stored readings within the app
- request deletion or correction of account-linked data through the app operator
